Present Tense -ARE verbs The present tense of a regular Italian verb in -are is formed by dropping the infinitive ending (-are) and adding the appropriate endings to the resulting root (-o, -i, -a, -iamo, -ate, -ano).

Example: Mangiare (to eat)

Present Tense -ERE verbs The present tense of a regular Italian verb in -ere is formed by dropping the infinitive ending (-ere) and adding the appropriate endings to the resulting root (-o, -i, -e, -iamo, -ete, -ono).

Example: Prendere (to take)

Present Tense -IRE verbs The present tense of a regular Italian verb in -ire is formed by dropping the infinitive ending (-ire) and adding the appropriate endings to the resulting root (-o, -i, -e, -iamo, -ite, -ono).

Example: Dormire (to sleep)
